DzidzaCode is the most efficient way for Zimbabwean students to connect with a mentor and begin their computing journey.

Sign Up Now »

Problem:

In the Zimbabwean language, Shona, the word for computer does not exist. Although students are curious, talented, and intelligent, they do not have the opportunity to code because they do not have mentors to look up to and resources specifically designed to meet their needs.

Story:

Last month, one of our co-founders, Pelagia Majoni, started an Introduction to Programming Boot Camp. She realized the immense need for mentorship, inspiration and resources for students to pursue their journey in tech.

Solution:

We created DzidzaCode to help Zimbabwean students connect with a mentor, learn how to code, and find professional development resources for their future careers in Computuer Science.

How Does it Work?

1. Create a Profile

Sign up using your email and add a profile picture so you can save your code.

2. Connect with a Mentor

Read the mentor descriptions and choose what mentor you would like to work with. Connect with the mentor to begin your curriculum. Chat with your mentor and view your assignments in the Mentorship tab.

3. Practice using Resources

Train your brain by using our free resources. Download textbooks, practice coding problems and enhance your skills. From Beginner to Advanced, these resources are helpful for any coder!

4. Complete Assignments

Submit your assignments to be reviewed by your mentor. Like taking a test, without any of the stress!

5. Get Mentor Feedback

Get feedback on your submitted assignments. Your mentor will message you with corrections, advise, and resources to help practice your skills.

6. Be a Computer Science Pro!

With our resources and mentor feedback, achieve your coding goals and strive for success!
